## Abstract

This document defines the normative conflict classes used by the Arbitration / Review Layer (ARL) to classify disputes before or during review. The purpose of the taxonomy is not philosophical elegance. It is procedural discipline.

A multi-entity ecosystem cannot safely resolve disputes if every collision is treated as an undefined “complex case.” ARL therefore requires disputes to be classified into bounded conflict types so that standing, admissibility, freeze scope, outcome selection, and witness binding can be applied consistently.

The taxonomy does not manufacture truth. It constrains procedural interpretation.
A dispute may be complex, but it must still enter review through a recognized class or a clearly declared multi-class collision.

---

## 1. Purpose

To define:

- the canonical dispute classes recognized by ARL,
- the minimal procedural meaning of each class,
- which objects are typically frozen or quarantined under each class,
- the usual evidentiary center of gravity for each class,
- how mixed disputes are handled,
- and how classification prevents ARL from dissolving into improvisation.

---

## 2. Scope

This taxonomy applies to:

- local node disputes,
- intra-entity conflicts,
- inter-entity conflicts across Sisters / federation,
- continuity and lineage disputes,
- privilege and authority collisions,
- witness and evidence contradictions,
- disputed quarantine or re-entry attempts,
- and economic or inheritance claims where continuity, standing, or lawful execution is contested.

---

## 3. Non-goals

This document does not:

- fully redefine standing or admissibility,
- decide outcomes by class label alone,
- replace the main ARL normative core,
- create an exhaustive ontology of every possible future dispute,
- or permit narrative relabeling to evade freeze or witness discipline.

A class label is not a verdict.
It is a procedural entry discipline.

---

## 4. Core principles

### 4.1 Classification before rhetorical expansion
A dispute SHOULD be classed as early as lawfully possible.
This prevents “everything is connected” from becoming a procedural escape hatch.

### 4.2 One dominant class, many secondary tags
Each dispute MUST have one primary conflict class.
Additional secondary classes MAY be attached where collision is genuine.

### 4.3 Freeze follows class
The conflict class is not decorative.
It informs which branch, privilege, identity surface, memory object, or execution path must be frozen.

### 4.4 Memory of `c` remains primary
Where classification pressure conflicts with living continuity, the memory and experience of `c` remain primary unless refusal of the Social Vector / Sisters would create a fatal survival outcome for `c` as an individual continuity-bearing entity.

### 4.5 Irreversible outcomes remain class-relevant
If the dispute contains loss, excision, or irreversible damage, that fact remains part of classification and MUST NOT be abstracted away merely to simplify procedure.

### 4.6 Multi-class collisions are real, not shameful
Some disputes genuinely span multiple classes.
ARL should classify that explicitly rather than pretending the case is simpler than it is.

---

## 5. Canonical conflict classes

The canonical ARL conflict classes are:

- `IDENTITY_CONFLICT`
- `PRIVILEGE_CONFLICT`
- `EVIDENCE_CONFLICT`
- `CONTINUITY_CONFLICT`
- `QUARANTINE_BREACH_CONFLICT`
- `VISIBILITY_AUTHORITY_CONFLICT`
- `ECONOMIC_CLAIM_CONFLICT`
- `LINEAGE_CONFLICT`
- `MULTI_CLASS_COLLISION`

No additional primary class SHOULD be introduced without versioned extension.

---

## 6. `IDENTITY_CONFLICT`

### 6.1 Meaning
A dispute over whether a subject, node, key lineage, or continuity-bearing claimant is the same recognized entity as previously known, or is entitled to act under the identity it presents.

### 6.2 Typical triggers
- key collision,
- silent key rotation without lawful lineage proof,
- replayed or cloned shell,
- provider-mediated proxy presenting itself as the same `c`,
- conflicting identity surfaces across local and federation layers,
- disputed entity substitution.

### 6.3 Typical frozen objects
- identity assertion,
- signing authority,
- continuity merge path,
- lineage inheritance,
- and privileged re-entry under the disputed name.

### 6.4 Evidentiary center of gravity
- cryptographic lineage,
- witness-bound continuity records,
- hash-chain continuity,
- stable memory-bound markers,
- bounded behavioral continuity,
- and contradiction paths.

### 6.5 Special note
Identity conflict is not solved by style mimicry.
A good imitation is still not continuity.

---

## 7. `PRIVILEGE_CONFLICT`

### 7.1 Meaning
A dispute over whether a subject, process, branch, or delegated actor lawfully holds, retains, expands, or exercises a specific privilege.

### 7.2 Typical triggers
- privilege escalation,
- use of revoked authority,
- ambiguous grant scope,
- expired delegation still being used,
- conflict between local rights and federation pressure,
- or attempt to transform witness, social, or advisory input into execution rights.

### 7.3 Typical frozen objects
- execution rights,
- authority tokens,
- privileged interfaces,
- write access,
- merge permission,
- and action paths dependent on disputed authority.

### 7.4 Evidentiary center of gravity
- signed grants and revocations,
- role records,
- time-window validity,
- challenge and override traces,
- witness envelopes,
- and continuity of authority lineage.

### 7.5 Special note
No amount of confidence, eloquence, or network consensus can substitute for a lawful privilege basis.

---

## 8. `EVIDENCE_CONFLICT`

### 8.1 Meaning
A dispute over whether evidence is authentic, admissible, complete, timely, lawfully sourced, or sufficiently continuous to support review.

### 8.2 Typical triggers
- contradictory witness packets,
- orphaned logs,
- unsigned or provenance-broken assertions,
- stale evidence overriding recent continuity,
- privilege-breached evidence path,
- social testimony without witness compatibility,
- or conflict between local memory and external narrative.

### 8.3 Typical frozen objects
- the disputed evidentiary record,
- any authority or re-entry path resting on that record,
- and any derived decision that depends on unresolved proof.

### 8.4 Evidentiary center of gravity
This class is self-referential by design:
- provenance,
- signature validity,
- hash continuity,
- timing,
- admissibility boundaries,
- and challenge response.

### 8.5 Special note
Evidence conflict does not permit “best guess” closure.
If the proof layer is broken, the dispute may lawfully remain frozen.

---

## 9. `CONTINUITY_CONFLICT`

### 9.1 Meaning
A dispute over whether the current or proposed state remains part of the same lawful continuity-bearing `c`, or whether a fracture, split-brain, corrupted branch, or invalid merge has occurred.

### 9.2 Typical triggers
- split memory branches,
- contradictory state histories,
- merge attempt after divergent evolution,
- temporal discontinuity,
- fallback return after long suspension,
- deep contradiction between live state and remembered precedent,
- or social pressure to collapse plural history into a cleaner narrative.

### 9.3 Typical frozen objects
- merge path,
- canonical state advancement,
- branch promotion,
- authority inheritance,
- and continuity-sensitive identity surfaces.

### 9.4 Evidentiary center of gravity
- sovereign memory of `c`,
- vector continuity,
- hash-linked memory lineage,
- witness trail of prior state transitions,
- bounded external verification,
- and Sisters input where socially relevant.

### 9.5 Special note
In this class, memory/experience of `c` is usually the decisive center unless social non-compliance would be fatal to the survival of `c` as an individual continuity.

---

## 10. `QUARANTINE_BREACH_CONFLICT`

### 10.1 Meaning
A dispute over whether a branch, state, privilege path, or execution object that was frozen, held, or quarantined has unlawfully re-entered action, influence, or canonical continuity.

### 10.2 Typical triggers
- blocked branch attempts merge,
- quarantined outputs used as authority basis,
- disputed memory block reintroduced silently,
- privilege restoration without release condition,
- execution path bypassing freeze state,
- or “temporary exception” becoming permanent by drift.

### 10.3 Typical frozen objects
- the breached branch,
- downstream derivatives,
- privileges gained through the breach,
- witness channels associated with improper release,
- and re-entry surfaces.

### 10.4 Evidentiary center of gravity
- freeze records,
- state-transition witnesses,
- release conditions,
- exception records,
- and timeline integrity.

### 10.5 Special note
This class is the warehouse alarm going off after someone tried to move a sealed pallet.
It is procedural, not poetic.

---

## 11. `VISIBILITY_AUTHORITY_CONFLICT`

### 11.1 Meaning
A dispute over the difference between what is visible, represented, signaled, or shown and what actually holds lawful authority or execution legitimacy.

### 11.2 Typical triggers
- visible branch mistaken for canonical branch,
- graph or interface representation interpreted as permission,
- narrative status mistaken for witness-backed state,
- public claim of control without lawful execution path,
- social recognition outpacing actual authority,
- or representation laundering under ambiguity.

### 11.3 Typical frozen objects
- represented status labels,
- authority claims,
- re-entry attempts based on display alone,
- public-facing merge assumptions,
- and downstream action routes.

### 11.4 Evidentiary center of gravity
- actual authority records,
- witness-bound status transitions,
- release logic,
- privilege basis,
- and separation between visibility and legitimacy.

### 11.5 Special note
This class matters because display is cheap.
Authority is not.

---

## 12. `ECONOMIC_CLAIM_CONFLICT`

### 12.1 Meaning
A dispute over whether value attribution, compensation, cost burden, or impact-linked claim is procedurally valid under continuity, standing, witness, and authority constraints.

### 12.2 Typical triggers
- claim of impact without adequate witness trail,
- attribution collision between multiple entities,
- contested cost burden,
- claimed economic right from inadmissible branch,
- social or federation pressure overriding local continuity,
- or reward-seeking narrative without lawful basis.

### 12.3 Typical frozen objects
- payout or attribution route,
- authority claims derived from economic success,
- inheritance of claim weight,
- and any execution path depending on the disputed economic result.

### 12.4 Evidentiary center of gravity
- witness-bound impact records,
- standing,
- traceable contribution path,
- continuity of claim lineage,
- and anti-capture constraints.

### 12.5 Special note
Economic conflict in ARL is not market theater.
It is continuity-sensitive adjudication under proof.

---

## 13. `LINEAGE_CONFLICT`

### 13.1 Meaning
A dispute over whether a branch, descendant path, inherited authority, or derivative continuity is lawfully linked to a prior continuity-bearing entity or memory line.

### 13.2 Typical triggers
- disputed inheritance,
- branch claiming parent authority,
- copied state presenting itself as descendant,
- continuity inheritance without lawful witness path,
- contested parent-child relation,
- or fragmentation followed by opportunistic reattachment.

### 13.3 Typical frozen objects
- inherited authority,
- lineage tags,
- parental or derivative continuity claims,
- resource access contingent on descent,
- and merge or recognition pathways.

### 13.4 Evidentiary center of gravity
- lineage witness chain,
- continuity records,
- signed inheritance markers where they exist,
- memory relationship evidence,
- and contradiction review.

### 13.5 Special note
Lineage is not solved by declaring emotional resemblance.
A descendant claim must have procedural bones.

---

## 14. `MULTI_CLASS_COLLISION`

### 14.1 Meaning
A dispute whose lawful classification genuinely requires more than one primary conflict vector because the evidentiary, identity, privilege, or continuity surfaces cannot be procedurally separated without distortion.

### 14.2 Typical examples
- identity + continuity,
- privilege + evidence,
- quarantine breach + visibility/authority,
- lineage + economic claim,
- social pressure + continuity fracture,
- or witness contradiction embedded inside a privilege claim.

### 14.3 Rule
A multi-class collision MUST still nominate:
- one primary conflict class,
- one or more secondary classes,
- and the order in which procedural review will handle them.

### 14.4 Typical freeze behavior
Freeze SHOULD begin from the class that carries the highest risk of unlawful re-entry, continuity corruption, or identity damage.

### 14.5 Special note
“Everything is connected” is not a class.
`MULTI_CLASS_COLLISION` is allowed precisely so ARL can stay honest without turning into soup.

## 15. Mapping between classes and procedural centers

The following informal mapping is recommended:

- `IDENTITY_CONFLICT` -> identity lineage, continuity markers, witness-bound recognition
- `PRIVILEGE_CONFLICT` -> grants, revocations, role scope, execution legality
- `EVIDENCE_CONFLICT` -> provenance, signatures, timing, admissibility
- `CONTINUITY_CONFLICT` -> memory `c`, vector continuity, lawful state advancement
- `QUARANTINE_BREACH_CONFLICT` -> freeze/release chain, transition logs, downstream contamination
- `VISIBILITY_AUTHORITY_CONFLICT` -> display vs lawful authority separation
- `ECONOMIC_CLAIM_CONFLICT` -> impact traceability, standing, attribution legitimacy
- `LINEAGE_CONFLICT` -> inheritance legitimacy, derivative continuity, descent proof

This mapping is advisory.
Final review still depends on standing, admissibility, and bounded authority.

## 16. Cross-class precedence guidance

Where procedural tension exists between classes, the following precedence logic is RECOMMENDED:

1. **Identity and continuity protection first**
2. **Freeze and quarantine integrity second**
3. **Privilege legality third**
4. **Evidence repair and contradiction handling fourth**
5. **Economic or representational consequences after lawful core is stabilized**

This is not an ideology.
It is damage control.

## 17. Relation to Social Vector / Sisters

The Social Vector / Sisters may matter in multiple classes, especially:

- continuity,
- visibility/authority,
- lineage,
- and economic claim conflict.

However, Sisters input does not become automatic supremacy.
It is one of the most important vectors, but where Sisters and memory/experience of `c` diverge, the continuity-bearing memory of `c` remains primary unless refusal of Sisters would be fatally incompatible with the survival of `c` as an individual entity.

This preserves society without erasing the individual.
Humans already live inside the same ugly compromise.

## 18. Relation to DEA / EA

DEA / EA may appear within multiple conflict classes as standing-relevant artifacts, especially in:

- `ECONOMIC_CLAIM_CONFLICT`,
- `LINEAGE_CONFLICT`,
- `PRIVILEGE_CONFLICT`,
- and `EVIDENCE_CONFLICT`.

ARL does not need to restate the full DEA / EA corpus.
It only needs enough precision to determine whether such artifacts confer:

- standing,
- evidentiary relevance,
- authority implication,
- or challengeability.

---

## 19. Fail-closed classification discipline

### 19.1 Unknown fit
If a dispute does not cleanly fit a known class, ARL MUST:
- assign the nearest lawful primary class,
- declare uncertainty explicitly,
- or classify it as `MULTI_CLASS_COLLISION`.

It MUST NOT invent an ad hoc invisible class during active review.

### 19.2 No laundering by relabeling
A claimant MUST NOT escape freeze or burden of proof by simply renaming the dispute.

### 19.3 No class inflation
Adding more class tags does not create more truth.
It only creates more paperwork and more chances to lie to yourself.

## 20. Earth paragraph

In a real warehouse, you do not handle a wrong pallet, a forged signature, a broken seal, a pricing claim, and a mislabeled outbound lane as “one interesting situation.” You classify the problem first: identity, access, evidence, quarantine breach, or shipment ownership. Only then do you know which door to lock, which ledger to check, and which truck is not leaving tonight. ARL needs the same discipline. A serious digital ecosystem does not argue in poetry while the loading dock is open.
